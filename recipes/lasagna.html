Description

Anyone who really knows me knows that I have a tradition of making lasagna for every polar vortex. What can I say, it's the perfect excuse to have the oven on for a while and eat rich food! This recipe is my go to. It's a spin on Alison Roman's recipe, but a little less work.

Ingredients

  • 16 oz dried lasagna noodles
  • 2 jars Trader Joes tomato sauce
  • 1 container whole milk riccotta
  • Like 1 cup of milk?? I don't remember
  • Some amount of mozzerlla, sorry
  • Optional add ons: spinach, mushrooms, meat of some kind, whatever

Steps

  1. If you're including any add ons, cook them first to release any water.
  2. Mix together the milk, ricotta, and mozzarella in a bowl. Reserva a little mozzarella.
  3. Apply a light layer of sauce at the bottom of your lasagna dish.
  4. Place the first layer of dried noodles across the dish, followed by about 3/4 cup of sauce, and a quarter of the cheese mixture. Add any other add ons. Repeat at least 4 times, or until out of cheese mixture.
  5. Place reserved moz on top. Maybe add some parm too if you have it and like extra crunch.
  6. Oh my god the oven! Go back to step 1 and preheat it to like 375. Anyway, place in oven covered for 45 min.
  7. After 45 min, finish cooking uncovered for another 35 min or until it's nice and brown.
  8. You really should let it cool for at least like 10 min before eating otherwise you'll burn your tongue.
